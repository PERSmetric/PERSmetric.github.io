Experimental Materials and Results of PERS Metric

Abstract:
Spectrum-based fault localization (SFL) techniques, which generally output a rank list of suspicious statements in descending order of their suspiciousness values, are initially proposed for assisting developers, and also extensively used in automated program repair tools in recent. Many evaluation metrics, including the common metric EXAM, have been introduced to evaluate and compare the effectiveness of SFL techniques. However, these metrics have one common limitation that they only focus on the rank of the faulty statement but ignore the accuracy of suspiciousness values, which can result in unfair comparison among SFL techniques.

In this paper we propose a new metric PERS to evaluate the effectiveness of SFL techniques, and the metric is particularly suitable to evaluate SFL from the viewpoint of automated program repair, a new application platform for SFL techniques. Unlike the EXAM metric which emphasizes the absolute rank of the faulty statement, PERS draws more attention to the accuracy of suspiciousness value of the faulty statement, thus scoring the techniques in accordance with the original intention of SFL techniques. We compare our metric against EXAM using 6 SFL techniques and 22 real-life bugs from Defects4j. Experimental results present the evidence that PERS has higher discrimination ability than EXAM, and the SFL technique with higher PERS has more significant assisting aids in automated program repair.
